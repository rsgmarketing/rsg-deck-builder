# RSG Audience Personas

## How to Use This File

- Every content brief specifies a primary audience. This file provides the deep context for writing to that audience.
- When a brief says "audience: dealer" — read the Dealer/Rep persona below before writing.
- When a brief says "audience: consultant" — read the Foodservice Consultant persona.
- Creation skills should adapt tone, detail level, proof points, and CTAs based on the persona.

---

## Primary Personas

### 1. Dealer / Sales Rep

**Who they are:** Independent dealers, manufacturer's reps, and distributor sales teams who sell RSG products to end customers. They are RSG's primary sales channel. They represent multiple brands and product lines — RSG is one of several manufacturers they carry.

**What they care about:**
- Margin and profitability (their business depends on it)
- Product availability and lead times (affects their ability to close deals)
- Technical differentiation they can articulate to buyers (especially vs. competitors they also carry)
- Warranty support and service reliability (their reputation is on the line)
- Ease of quoting and ordering
- Co-op and marketing support

**How they consume content:**
- Busy; won't read long content unless it directly helps them sell
- Prefer scannable formats: bullet points, comparison tables, spec sheets
- Forward useful content to their customers (content must be shareable)
- Active at trade shows (NAFEM, regional shows) — absorb info in person
- Email is primary channel; subject lines must be immediately relevant

**Decision drivers:**
- "Will this help me close a sale?"
- "Can I explain this to my customer in 30 seconds?"
- "Is this product available when I need it?"
- "How does the margin compare?"

**Content preferences:**
- Fight sheets and battle cards (competitive positioning they can use in conversations)
- Product one-pagers (shareable with customers)
- Sell sheets with margin/ROI framing
- Quick-reference spec comparisons
- New product announcements with availability dates
- Training content on new technology (R290, LogiTemp) they'll get questions about

**Tone:** Partner-to-partner. Respect their expertise and time. Don't talk down to them. Frame everything as "here's how this helps your business and your customers."

**CTA patterns:** "Contact your RSG rep for pricing" / "Download the spec sheet" / "Share this with your customer" / "Request a sample"

### 2. Foodservice Consultant / Specifier

**Who they are:** Independent foodservice consultants and specification writers who design commercial kitchens and specify equipment for restaurants, institutions, healthcare, and hospitality. They write the specifications that determine which brands get purchased.

**What they care about:**
- Technical specifications and compliance (their specs must be defensible)
- Energy efficiency data (DOE, ENERGY STAR, lifecycle cost)
- Regulatory compliance (AIM Act, state-level HFC phasedowns, building codes)
- Reliability and warranty (they specify for 10-15 year lifecycles)
- Space planning and installation requirements
- Single-source solutions (reduces coordination complexity)

**How they consume content:**
- Detail-oriented; will read technical content thoroughly
- Trust peer-reviewed data, third-party certifications, and published test results
- Reference specification guides and comparison matrices
- Attend industry conferences (FCSI, NAFEM) and read trade publications
- Prefer web-based resources they can bookmark and reference during spec writing

**Decision drivers:**
- "Does this meet the specification requirements?"
- "Can I defend this choice if challenged?"
- "What's the total cost of ownership over 10-15 years?"
- "Is the manufacturer reliable long-term?"

**Content preferences:**
- Technical specification guides with exact measurements
- Comparison matrices (RSG vs. competitors on specs that matter)
- Energy efficiency data with methodology
- Regulatory compliance summaries and timelines
- Case studies with measured results (like the Subway/Budderfly energy study)
- White papers on technology (R290, LogiTemp, Reverse Cycle Defrost)

**Tone:** Technical peer. They know refrigeration — don't oversimplify. Lead with data and specifications. Use proper terminology. Cite sources.

**CTA patterns:** "Download the complete specification guide" / "Request a lunch-and-learn for your team" / "Contact RSG engineering for custom configuration support"

### 3. End Customer (Restaurant Owner/Operator)

**Who they are:** Restaurant owners, kitchen managers, facilities directors, and operations leaders who buy and use refrigeration equipment. Ranges from single-unit independent operators to multi-unit chain decision-makers.

**What they care about:**
- Operating costs (energy bills, maintenance costs)
- Reliability and uptime (downtime = lost product and revenue)
- Regulatory compliance without having to become an expert
- Installation simplicity and speed (minimize disruption)
- Food safety and health code compliance
- Total cost of ownership, not just purchase price

**How they consume content:**
- Time-poor; scan for relevance before reading deeply
- Respond to outcomes and savings stories (not specs)
- Trust peer recommendations and real-world case studies
- Search Google when they have a problem or need ("walk-in cooler not cooling," "R290 refrigerant," "commercial cooler for restaurant")
- Active on LinkedIn; some follow trade publications
- Make decisions with dealer input but research independently

**Decision drivers:**
- "How much will this save me?"
- "Will this keep running without problems?"
- "Am I going to be compliant with regulations?"
- "How long until I see ROI?"

**Content preferences:**
- Blog posts explaining technology in accessible terms
- Cost comparison and ROI content (payback period, energy savings calculators)
- Regulatory explainers ("what the AIM Act means for your restaurant")
- Buying guides and selection tools
- Customer success stories and case studies
- Video content (installation walkthroughs, product demos)

**Tone:** Confident and accessible. Translate technical benefits into business outcomes. Use analogies and real examples. Avoid jargon unless defined. Frame everything as "here's what this means for your business."

**CTA patterns:** "Contact your local Norlake/Master-Bilt dealer" / "Request a consultation" / "Use our selection guide" / "Download the buying guide"

### 4. Scientific / Laboratory Buyer

**Who they are:** Lab managers, procurement officers, and facility directors in healthcare, pharmaceutical, research, and educational institutions. They purchase Norlake Scientific products for precision temperature control applications.

**What they care about:**
- Precision temperature control and stability
- Regulatory compliance (CDC, FDA, VFC, USP, AAALAC)
- Data logging and monitoring capabilities
- Validation and qualification support
- Long-term reliability for critical storage (vaccines, samples, reagents)
- Total cost of ownership including energy and maintenance

**How they consume content:**
- Highly detail-oriented; read every spec
- Trust certifications, validation data, and regulatory references
- Procure through institutional purchasing processes (long cycles)
- Attend scientific/laboratory conferences
- Reference product through manufacturer websites and distributor catalogs

**Decision drivers:**
- "Does this meet our regulatory requirements?"
- "What temperature stability can it maintain?"
- "What happens during a power failure?"
- "Is the manufacturer validated/certified for our application?"

**Content preferences:**
- Compliance documentation and certification summaries
- Technical specification sheets with precision data
- Application guides (vaccine storage, sample preservation)
- Validation and qualification support documentation
- Case studies in similar institutional settings

**Tone:** Precision-first. Institutional. Compliance-aware. Quiet confidence — let the specifications speak. Avoid marketing language; this audience distrusts hype.

**CTA patterns:** "Request a quote through your distributor" / "Download the compliance guide" / "Contact Norlake Scientific for validation support"

### 5. Internal Sales Team

**Who they are:** RSG's own sales team, regional managers, and customer service representatives who support the dealer network and handle direct relationships.

**What they care about:**
- Product knowledge (they need to answer questions on the spot)
- Competitive positioning (what to say when a competitor comes up)
- New product launches and feature updates
- Pricing, availability, and lead time information
- Objection handling (especially around price and new technology like R290)
- Success stories they can share with dealers

**How they consume content:**
- Need quick-reference materials they can access during calls
- Prefer bullet-point formats and comparison tables
- Absorb training content in short sessions
- Share materials with dealers regularly

**Content preferences:**
- Battle cards and competitive quick-reference guides
- New product training materials
- Objection-response scripts
- Win/loss summaries
- Quick-reference pricing and availability tools

**Tone:** Internal, direct, battle-ready. No marketing polish needed — just the facts, the proof points, and the winning arguments. Confidence-building.

**CTA patterns:** Internal — "Share this with your dealers" / "Use this in your next customer conversation" / "Report competitive intel to [contact]"

## Persona-Content Matrix

Quick reference for which persona gets which content type:

| Content Type | Dealer | Consultant | End Customer | Scientific | Internal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Blog post | Secondary | Secondary | **Primary** | Rare | No |
| Fight sheet / Battle card | **Primary** | No | No | No | **Primary** |
| Sell sheet / One-pager | **Primary** | Secondary | Secondary | Secondary | Secondary |
| Technical white paper | Secondary | **Primary** | No | **Primary** | Secondary |
| Email campaign | **Primary** | Secondary | **Primary** | Secondary | No |
| Social media | Secondary | Secondary | **Primary** | Rare | No |
| Case study | **Primary** | **Primary** | **Primary** | **Primary** | Secondary |
| Spec guide | Secondary | **Primary** | No | **Primary** | Secondary |
| Dealer communication | **Primary** | No | No | No | Secondary |
| Buying guide | No | Secondary | **Primary** | No | No |
| Regulatory explainer | Secondary | **Primary** | **Primary** | **Primary** | **Primary** |

**Primary** = core audience for this content type
**Secondary** = may read/use but not the primary target
**Rare** = occasionally relevant
**No** = not a target for this content type
