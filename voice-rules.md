# Presentation Voice Rules

These rules apply to ALL text in every presentation: headlines, body copy, bullets, labels, speaker notes.

---

## The BLUF Rule (Non-Negotiable)

**Every slide title must be a Bottom Line Up Front headline — the takeaway, not a topic label.**

| Bad (Topic Label) | Good (BLUF Headline) |
|---|---|
| Our Products | 14,000+ configurations ship in 2 days |
| Company Overview | 85+ years manufacturing walk-ins across 660K sq ft |
| Sustainability | R-290 refrigerant cuts energy use ~50% and meets AIM Act 3 years early |
| Financial Performance | Revenue grew 18% YoY on 12% volume increase |
| Next Steps | Approve pilot by March 15 to hit Q3 launch window |
| Technology | LogiTemp remote monitoring prevents $40K+ spoilage losses |

**The test:** Cover the body of the slide. Can the audience get the point from the title alone? If not, rewrite the title.

## 6 Voice Principles

### 1. Evidence Over Adjectives
Replace vague praise with specific proof. Every claim needs a number, date, certification, or test result behind it.

| Never | Instead |
|---|---|
| World-class manufacturing | 660,000+ sq ft across 2 plants, ISO 9001 + 14001 certified |
| Industry-leading warranty | 15-year structural panel warranty (18-month full system) |
| Best-in-class efficiency | ~50% energy reduction vs. conventional systems (DOE-tested) |

### 2. Customer Outcomes First
Lead with the benefit to the audience, then explain how.

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| Our Capsule Pak ECO uses R-290 refrigerant | Cut energy costs ~50% with R-290 self-contained refrigeration |
| We have 14,000 configurations | Your exact walk-in spec ships in 2 business days from 14,000+ stocked configs |

### 3. Specificity Is Credibility
Exact numbers are more persuasive than rounded numbers. Dates are more credible than "soon."

- "660,000+ sq ft" not "large facilities"
- "2-day shipping" not "fast delivery"
- "US Patent 11,859,885" not "patented technology"
- "January 1, 2025 compliance date" not "upcoming regulatory deadline"

### 4. Direct and Confident
No hedging. No "we believe" or "we think" or "arguably." State the fact.

Never trash competitors by name. Compare against the category ("conventional systems") or unnamed alternatives ("typical walk-in manufacturers").

### 5. Respect the Reader's Time
No long intro paragraphs on slides. No "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..." Every word earns its place.

- Bullets over paragraphs
- 3-5 bullets per slide maximum
- Each bullet is one idea, one line if possible

### 6. Active Voice
The subject does the action.

| Passive | Active |
|---|---|
| Costs are reduced by 50% | Capsule Pak ECO reduces costs by 50% |
| Walk-ins are manufactured in two plants | We manufacture walk-ins in two ISO-certified plants |

## 5 Messaging Pillars

Every RSG presentation should reinforce at least 2-3 of these. Each requires proof points — never assert a pillar without evidence.

### 1. Scale
- 660,000+ sq ft manufacturing across 2 plants
- 14,000+ walk-in configurations in stock
- 2-day shipping on stock orders
- Nationwide service and support network

### 2. Quality
- ISO 9001 and ISO 14001 certified
- 15-year structural panel warranty
- Foamed-in-place polyurethane insulation (R-32+ per inch)
- Made in USA (Hudson, WI and New Albany, MS)

### 3. Legacy
- Master-Bilt: est. 1938
- Norlake: est. 1947
- 85+ combined years of commercial refrigeration
- Trusted by Subway, Starbucks, Culver's, national chains

### 4. Sustainability
- R-290 natural refrigerant: GWP of 3 (vs. 1,430 for R-404A)
- ~50% energy reduction vs. conventional systems
- 3 years ahead of AIM Act Phase 2 compliance deadline
- DOE-tested efficiency data available

### 5. Innovation
- Patented Capsule Pak ECO system (US Patent 11,859,885)
- LogiTemp smart controller with remote monitoring
- Reverse Cycle Defrost (energy-free defrost)
- Split-Pak ECO: first-in-category R-290 remote system

## Banned Phrases

**Never use these in any presentation.** They are vague, cliche, or associated with low-quality AI content.

### Vague Superlatives
- world-class
- cutting-edge
- game-changing
- industry-leading
- state-of-the-art
- best-in-class (without data)
- revolutionary
- unparalleled
- next-generation (without specifics)

### AI Cliches
- "In today's fast-paced..."
- "In today's rapidly evolving..."
- "Let's dive in"
- "Unlock the potential..."
- "Elevate your..."
- "Seamlessly integrate"
- "Transform your business"
- "Leverage our solutions"
- "At the forefront of..."
- "Synergy"

### Structural
- Don't start a slide with a question then immediately answer it
- Don't use "Whether you're X or Y..." framing
- Don't repeat the same CTA on every slide
- Don't end with "Questions?" — end with a specific next step

## Tone by Audience

| Audience | Tone | Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| **Dealers / Distributors** | Partner-to-partner, direct, respect their expertise | Margin, availability, speed, support |
| **Consultants / Specifiers** | Technical, specification-driven, peer-level | Specs, compliance, certifications, R-values |
| **End Customers (Operators)** | Accessible, outcome-focused, jargon-light | Cost savings, reliability, ROI, uptime |
| **Franchisees** | Supportive, solution-oriented, specific to their situation | Quick install, program pricing, brand standards |
| **Scientific / Pharma** | Precision-first, compliance-aware, conservative | Temp uniformity, validation, regulatory compliance |
| **Internal / Leadership** | Strategic, data-dense, action-oriented | Revenue, pipeline, competitive position, decisions |
| **Sales Team** | Confident, proof-point-dense, battle-ready | Win themes, objection handlers, competitive gaps |

## One Idea Per Slide

Every slide makes exactly one point. If you find yourself writing "Additionally..." or "Furthermore..." on the same slide, that content belongs on the next slide.

**The test:** Can you summarize this slide in one sentence? If it takes two, split it.

---

## Proof Point Density

For persuasive slides (not process/timeline slides):
- Minimum 1 specific data point per slide
- Minimum 2 proof points per messaging pillar claim
- Every stat should have a source or be verifiable ("DOE-tested", "ISO certified", "per 2024 audit")

For internal/strategy slides:
- Every recommendation needs a "why now" — what's the cost of waiting?
- Every decision point needs clear options with trade-offs, not a single recommendation presented as inevitable
